Come Take a Look Behind the Red Door

In feng shui, a red door signifies “welcome,” a place where energy enters. The color red means energy and strength, power and passion. It means action and is often a differentiator. When you take that strength and passion, and incorporate it into a strategic-advocacy engagement, you can create action for your organization. These “red-door” strategies which I share in my new book, Behind the Red Door: Unlock Your Advocacy Influence and Success, are the tools and tactics needed to help you create effective advocacy efforts. So enter and see what kind of success awaits you behind the red door of advocacy.




Advocacy: More Than a Tool
on “The Hill”

Advocacy is no longer just a tactic used in political campaigns; we’ve moved beyond “pass a bill; kill a bill.”

So what does that mean?

Today, it is about solving issues and affecting change – inspiring collaboration between nontraditional allies who, even though they may not agree on everything, are coming together to accomplish mutually beneficial goals. It’s highlighting issues and supporting causes; it’s integrating advocacy tactics with marketing strategies designed to influence decision makers and to change local, state, and federal policy and practice. That’s the differentiator.

Leveraging a New Set of
Tools and Strategies

In my twenty years of experience developing advocacy campaigns and working with organizations, ranging from not-for-profits to Fortune 500 companies, we’ve seen emerging trends that are shaping today’s advocacy landscape. Advocacy should be a required asset for any organization trying to make its mark on the world. Done well, advocacy can further your organization’s efforts to achieve its goals and objectives – making it an essential resource in every toolbox.

Behind the Red Door: Unlock Your Advocacy Influence and Success

Whether you run a small not-for-profit organization or are the VP of a corporation, this book will help you understand how advocacy can be an effective strategy to add to your toolbox and help you prepare an advocacy plan that moves your target audiences into meaningful action. Behind the Red Door walks you through a comprehensive look at advocacy including building an advocacy-plan framework, creating and utilizing a brand, building and mobilizing internal and external stakeholders, using social media, dealing with crisis communications, and finally, measuring results.
